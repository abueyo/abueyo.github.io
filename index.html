About Me

I am a PhD Candidate at the University of Aberdeen. My research focuses on Explainable and Bio-inspired AI Approaches to Navigation in Autonomous Robotic Agents.

I obtained my bachelor's degree in Electronics Engineering from the University of Nigeria Nsukka and my master's degree in Information Technology from the University of the West of England, Bristol.

My over 10 years of working experience is varied and cuts across oil and gas, IT, and consulting. In my current role as a Business Advisor in the Upstream Deepwater Gas Resource Team of the Nigerian National Petroleum Company Limited, I advise on the technical and commercial aspects of Upstream Deepwater Gas Investments. I previously worked in the same organization as a Surface Facilities Engineer (Electrical and Instrumentation).

Research

My research lies at the intersection of explainable AI and bio-inspired algorithms, with a focus on navigation in autonomous systems:

Explainable AI

Developing transparent AI systems that can explain their decision-making processes

Bio-Inspired AI

Applying nature-inspired algorithms to solve complex computational problems

Autonomous AI

Creating self-sufficient intelligent systems capable of operating in dynamic environments

Robotic Navigation

Developing algorithms for efficient and safe robot movement in complex environments
